Cobalt(II) sulfate is any of the inorganic compounds with the formula CoSO4(H2O)x. Usually cobalt sulfate refers to the hexa- or heptahydrates CoSO4.6H2O or CoSO4.7H2O, respectively.[1] The heptahydrate is a red solid that is soluble in water and methanol. Since cobalt(II) has an odd number of electrons, it salts are paramagnetic.
